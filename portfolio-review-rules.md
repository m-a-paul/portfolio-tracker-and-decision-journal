# Portfolio Review Rules

Use these rules whenever reviewing the portfolio, evaluating new trades, or discussing cash management.

## Cash Dashboard

Every portfolio review should include:

- Total cash
- Exercise reserve for realistic bought-call candidates
- Free cash after exercise reserve

Formula:

```text
Free cash = Total cash - Exercise reserve
```

The exercise reserve should focus on calls the user might realistically want to exercise and hold. Exclude obvious lottery tickets or positions the user explicitly says to ignore, such as BMNR and GRAB unless that changes.

## Cash Discipline

- Do not treat new cash as automatically deployable.
- New cash should first reduce pressure and improve optionality.
- Maintain dry powder for dips and assignment risk.
- Covered call, cash secured put, and long-call decisions should be evaluated against free cash, not just total cash.

## Long Calls

The user may not intend to exercise every long call. If a position runs, partial sales or post-exercise share trims may be part of the plan.

When reviewing long calls, distinguish:

- Full exercise cash requirement
- Realistic exercise reserve
- Potential partial-sale/trim plan
